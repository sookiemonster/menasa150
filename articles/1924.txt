   <p>After working in the peddling business, many Arab Americans moved to entrepreneurial endeavors. Among them, the textile and garment industries were among the most popular choices. Although there was community pressure for Syrian women to marry and bear children, and many gladly did so, some Syrian women continued working in the peddling business after the 1930s. Generally speaking, though Syrians were not in control of a prominent business sector, they entered politics, involving themselves in everything from local elections to the president's cabinet.</p>
